The Life of a Champion

*Muhammed Ali, born Cassius Clay on January 17, 1942, was an American boxer and global icon. Known for his speed and charisma, Ali became a three-time World Heavyweight Champion.

*Key events in his career include his famous "Fight of the Century" against Joe Frazier, *"The Rumble in the Jungle" against George Foreman, and "The Thrilla in Manila" with Frazier.

*In 1964, at age 22, he won the World Heavyweight title and announced his conversion to Islam, changing his name to Muhammad Ali, rejecting his "slave name" Cassius Clay.

*He refused to be drafted into theVietnam War in 1967, citing his religious beliefs, and was stripped of his boxing titles. *After a legal battle, he returned to the ring in 1970.

*Ali's courage, activism, and boxing excellence made him a worldwide symbol of resistance and justice.

*He passed away on June 3, 2016, leaving a legacy that extends beyond sports, inspiring generations.
